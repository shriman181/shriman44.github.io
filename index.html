The Founder

Built By Someone
Who Knows
Your Floor.

"I've watched print shops with decades of craft lose brand contracts because they couldn't generate a sustainability PDF. That's not a skills problem — that's a tools problem. Oblique is the tool they should have had ten years ago."

Harshit Sharma grew up with a first-hand understanding of what really happens behind a print job ticket — the substrate calculations done in a ledger, the last-minute proof change arriving at 11 PM via WhatsApp, the estimator who carries three years of margin knowledge entirely in memory. That proximity to the operational reality of Indian print shops became the founding insight for Oblique.

Before founding Oblique, Harshit identified a structural gap that no software company had seriously addressed: Indian print and packaging businesses collectively handle hundreds of crores in substrate spend and supply some of the world's largest FMCG and pharma brands — yet the software layer governing their daily operations had not meaningfully evolved in over a decade. Legacy MIS tools were priced for large enterprise, built for Western markets, and designed before AI existed as a commercial reality.

Oblique is the convergence of that industry intimacy with modern AI engineering — purpose-built for the 2.5 lakh printing and packaging establishments across India, with the ambition to become the global operating standard for the industry over the next decade.
